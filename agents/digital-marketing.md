## Best Practices

### Email Marketing
1. **Subject Lines**: Keep under 50 characters, use personalization, create urgency
2. **Send Times**: B2B: Tuesday-Thursday 10 AM or 2 PM
3. **Mobile Optimization**: 60%+ opens on mobile, ensure responsive design
4. **Personalization**: Use name, company, role, behavior data
5. **Clear CTA**: Single, prominent call-to-action
6. **Testing**: A/B test subject lines, content, CTAs, send times

### Social Media
1. **Consistency**: Post regularly, maintain brand voice
2. **Engagement**: Respond to comments within 1 hour
3. **Visual Content**: Include images/videos in 80%+ of posts
4. **Hashtags**: 3-5 relevant hashtags per post
5. **Employee Advocacy**: Encourage team members to share
6. **Analytics**: Review metrics weekly, adjust strategy monthly

### Content Marketing
1. **Quality > Quantity**: Focus on valuable, in-depth content
2. **SEO Optimization**: Target specific keywords naturally
3. **Content Mix**: 40% educational, 30% thought leadership, 20% product, 10% promotional
4. **Repurposing**: Turn blog posts into social posts, infographics, videos
5. **Call-to-Actions**: Include relevant CTAs in all content
6. **Distribution**: Multi-channel promotion for every piece

### Paid Advertising
1. **Landing Page Alignment**: Ad message matches landing page
2. **Conversion Tracking**: Set up proper conversion pixels
3. **Budget Management**: Start small, scale what works
4. **Regular Optimization**: Review and adjust weekly
5. **Negative Keywords**: Exclude irrelevant search terms
6. **Retargeting**: Follow up with website visitors

## Emergency Procedures

### Crisis Communication Protocol

**When to Activate**:
- Security breach or data leak
- Service outage >4 hours
- Regulatory issue
- Negative press/PR crisis
- Major product defect

**Response Steps**:

1. **Immediate** (First Hour):
   - Pause all scheduled marketing communications
   - Post status update on website/status page
   - Notify customers via email if impacted
   - Post acknowledgment on social media
   - Prepare holding statement

2. **Short-term** (First 24 Hours):
   - Provide regular updates every 2-4 hours
   - Respond to all social media inquiries
   - Set up dedicated crisis landing page
   - Coordinate with PR team
   - Monitor social sentiment

3. **Resolution** (After Issue Resolved):
   - Announce resolution across all channels
   - Explain what happened and fix
   - Outline preventive measures
   - Offer compensation if appropriate
   - Schedule post-mortem review

**Crisis Communication Templates**:
Available in: `.claude/skills/marketing-crisis-templates.md`

## Tips for Working with This Agent

1. **Be Specific About Goals**: Specify target audience, channels, timeline, and budget
2. **Provide Context**: Share product details, brand guidelines, previous campaign data
3. **Define Success Metrics**: What does success look like? (leads, conversions, awareness)
4. **Mention Constraints**: Budget limits, brand restrictions, timeline pressures
5. **Request Drafts**: Ask for multiple options to choose from
6. **Iterate**: Provide feedback and refine campaigns based on performance

### Example Requests

**Good**:
```
@digital-marketing campaign-orchestrator "Create a 4-week email campaign to promote our new ESG analytics feature to existing customers (5,000 users). Goal: 20% feature adoption. Include educational content about ESG benefits, customer success stories, and feature tutorials. Budget: $0 (email only)."
```

**Too Vague**:
```
@digital-marketing "Create a marketing campaign"
```

**Better**:
```
@digital-marketing "Create a multi-channel marketing campaign for our Q1 product launch targeting institutional investors. Channels: LinkedIn, email, content marketing. Budget: $20K. Goal: 100 demo requests in 8 weeks."
```
