## Overview

The Employee Onboarding Module ensures every new hire has a smooth, efficient, and comprehensive onboarding experience. It covers:

✅ **Documentation Management** - E-signatures, compliance documents, handbook
✅ **Training Coordination** - Role-specific training paths, compliance training
✅ **System Provisioning** - Email, Slack, GitHub, JIRA, AWS, VPN access
✅ **Workflow Management** - 30-60-90 day plans, checklists, milestones
✅ **Compliance & Legal** - I-9, tax forms, background checks, agreements
✅ **Manager Support** - Check-in scheduling, expectations, success metrics
✅ **Team Integration** - Buddy system, team introductions, culture immersion

---

## Core Onboarding Framework

### Three-Phase Onboarding Approach

#### Phase 1: Pre-Onboarding (Offer Acceptance → Day 1)
**Duration**: 2-4 weeks
**Owner**: HR + Hiring Manager
**Goal**: Complete preparation and excitement

**Key Activities**:
- Welcome communication and start date confirmation
- Equipment ordering and setup
- Account creation (email, Slack, GitHub)
- Document collection (e-signatures)
- Team preparation and buddy assignment
- Manager check-in scheduling

#### Phase 2: Day 1-30 (Integration & Foundation)
**Duration**: 30 days
**Owner**: Manager + Buddy + HR
**Goal**: Team integration and role foundation

**Key Activities**:
- Company and team orientation
- System and tool training
- Department deep-dives
- 1:1 meetings with team members
- Project assignment and context
- Check-ins with manager

#### Phase 3: Day 31-90 (Ramp-Up & Performance)
**Duration**: 60 days
**Owner**: Manager + Team
**Goal**: Full productivity and independence

**Key Activities**:
- Complex project ownership
- Skill development and training
- Performance feedback
- Team culture participation
- 30-day evaluation and adjustment
- 60-day milestone assessment
- 90-day final evaluation

## Pre-Onboarding (Offer to Start Date)

### Pre-Day 1 Checklist (2-4 weeks before start)

**HR Responsibilities**:
- [ ] Send welcome email with start date confirmation
- [ ] Request required documents (ID, banking info, emergency contacts)
- [ ] Send offer letter and employment agreement for e-signature
- [ ] Send NDA and confidentiality agreement
- [ ] Create employee record in HR system
- [ ] Process I-9 documentation (collect day 1)
- [ ] Schedule background check
- [ ] Prepare employee handbook and policies

**IT Responsibilities**:
- [ ] Order laptop and monitor
- [ ] Order phone and accessories
- [ ] Set up office space/desk
- [ ] Create email account (firstname.lastname@aurigraph.io)
- [ ] Create Slack account
- [ ] Prepare hardware for delivery/setup

**Manager Responsibilities**:
- [ ] Meet with HR to confirm start date and role details
- [ ] Review job description and expectations
- [ ] Identify onboarding buddy (team member with similar background)
- [ ] Schedule day-1 welcome meeting
- [ ] Prepare team introduction materials
- [ ] Assign initial projects/context for learning

**Finance Responsibilities**:
- [ ] Collect W-4 and tax documents (via HR)
- [ ] Set up payroll
- [ ] Prepare benefits enrollment materials

## Day 1 Onboarding

### Day 1 Schedule (8-10 hours)

**Morning (Arrival & Welcome)**:
- [ ] 8:30 AM - Welcome by manager and buddy
- [ ] 8:45 AM - Office tour and desk setup
- [ ] 9:00 AM - Coffee/breakfast with buddy
- [ ] 9:30 AM - HR orientation (30 min)
  - Welcome from HR
  - Benefits overview
  - Payroll setup
  - Handbook review
  - I-9 completion
- [ ] 10:15 AM - IT setup (30 min)
  - Email account access
  - Slack/Teams setup
  - Password manager setup
  - Hardware distribution

**Midday (Company Orientation)**:
- [ ] 11:00 AM - Company overview presentation (30 min)
  - Company mission and values
  - Key products and markets
  - Organizational structure
  - Success stories and culture
- [ ] 11:30 AM - Team lunch with buddy and team (1 hour)
- [ ] 12:30 PM - Initial 1:1 with manager (30 min)
  - Role expectations and goals
  - Success criteria for 30/60/90 days
  - Current priorities and projects
  - Questions and concerns

**Afternoon (Systems & Training)**:
- [ ] 1:15 PM - Technology systems training (45 min)
  - Email, calendar, Slack orientation
  - GitHub access and repository setup
  - JIRA/project management intro
  - Common tools and shortcuts
  - Security and password practices
- [ ] 2:00 PM - Department overview (30 min)
  - Department purpose and structure
  - Key metrics and goals
  - Recent wins and challenges
- [ ] 2:30 PM - Break and informal time
- [ ] 2:45 PM - Meet team members (1:1s) (45 min total)
  - 15 min each with 3 key team members
  - Learn about their roles and personalities
  - Get questions about team culture
- [ ] 3:30 PM - Manager check-in and wrap-up (15 min)
  - How are you feeling?
  - What questions do you have?
  - Confirm tomorrow's schedule

**After Hours**:
- [ ] Team dinner/social gathering (if applicable)
- [ ] Informal introduction to company culture
- [ ] Connection with buddy for after-work activities

### Day 1 Success Criteria
✅ New hire feels welcomed and safe
✅ All systems access provisioned and working
✅ Basic orientation completed
✅ Understanding of role and expectations
✅ Positive first impression of company culture

## Week 1 Integration

### Week 1 Schedule (30-40 hours of structured activity)

**Daily Standup** (10 min daily):
- [ ] Join daily standup with team
- [ ] Observe how team communicates
- [ ] Get comfortable with format and pace

**Training Sessions** (15-20 hours):
- [ ] Monday: Company and product deep-dive (2 hours)
- [ ] Tuesday: Department systems and workflows (2 hours)
- [ ] Wednesday: GitHub and code repository orientation (2 hours)
- [ ] Thursday: JIRA, testing, and QA processes (2 hours)
- [ ] Friday: Company culture and team activities (1 hour)

**1:1 Meetings** (30 min each):
- [ ] Daily manager check-in (10 min)
- [ ] Meet 5 more team members (2.5 hours total)
- [ ] Meet 2 cross-functional partners (1 hour)

**Learning Activities**:
- [ ] Read company handbook and policies
- [ ] Review department standards and procedures
- [ ] Complete compliance training (2-4 hours)
- [ ] Review code of conduct and security policies
- [ ] Complete ethics and anti-harassment training

**Initial Assignment**:
- [ ] Receive "starter task" or documentation task
- [ ] Set up development environment (if technical role)
- [ ] Contribute to code or documentation (guidance provided)
- [ ] Deliver small wins to build confidence

### Week 1 Success Criteria
✅ Understands company culture and values
✅ Knows how systems and workflows operate
✅ Has built relationships with key team members
✅ Feels confident in initial assignments
✅ Completed all required training and compliance

## 30-Day Ramp-Up

### Goals for First 30 Days

**Learning Goals**:
- [ ] Understand company strategy and competitive landscape
- [ ] Learn department role and responsibilities
- [ ] Master key systems and tools
- [ ] Understand code/process standards
- [ ] Complete all onboarding training

**Relationship Goals**:
- [ ] Build strong relationship with manager
- [ ] Develop rapport with team members
- [ ] Understand dynamics and personalities
- [ ] Find your place in team culture
- [ ] Identify mentors or guides

**Performance Goals**:
- [ ] Complete assigned starter projects
- [ ] Submit first code/work contributions
- [ ] Participate in meetings confidently
- [ ] Ask good questions and learn
- [ ] Deliver small wins

**Integration Goals**:
- [ ] Attend department meetings
- [ ] Participate in team activities
- [ ] Understand informal processes
- [ ] Know who to ask for help
- [ ] Participate in company culture

### Day 30 Checkpoint

**30-Day Review Meeting** (with manager, 30-45 min):
- [ ] Review progress against expectations
- [ ] Discuss what's going well
- [ ] Identify challenges and blockers
- [ ] Adjust 60-90 day plan as needed
- [ ] Confirm understanding of role
- [ ] Set new goals for next 30 days

**30-Day Evaluation Criteria**:
- ✅ Foundational knowledge of role and company
- ✅ Technical setup complete and productive
- ✅ Positive relationships with team
- ✅ Initial contributions completed
- ✅ No major concerns or issues

## 60-Day Milestone

### Goals for Days 31-60

**Growing Independence**:
- [ ] Take on more complex projects
- [ ] Work with less supervision
- [ ] Mentor newer team members (if applicable)
- [ ] Own parts of projects
- [ ] Make independent decisions

**Skill Development**:
- [ ] Deepen technical knowledge
- [ ] Learn advanced systems and processes
- [ ] Understand business context
- [ ] Develop domain expertise
- [ ] Complete role-specific training

**Performance Building**:
- [ ] Deliver measurable results
- [ ] Reduce ramp-up time for future tasks
- [ ] Increase quality and speed
- [ ] Take initiative on improvements
- [ ] Demonstrate problem-solving

**Team Contribution**:
- [ ] Full participation in team projects
- [ ] Contribution to team goals
- [ ] Help improve processes
- [ ] Share ideas and perspectives
- [ ] Build strong team relationships

### Day 60 Checkpoint

**60-Day Assessment** (with manager, 30-45 min):
- [ ] Review performance against goals
- [ ] Discuss strengths and development areas
- [ ] Provide constructive feedback
- [ ] Identify skill gaps and training
- [ ] Plan final 30 days (days 61-90)
- [ ] Confirm role fit and expectations

## 90-Day Evaluation

### Day 90 Final Evaluation

**Formal Review** (with manager and HR, 60 min):

**Performance Assessment**:
- [ ] Technical proficiency in role
- [ ] Quality of work and contributions
- [ ] Speed and productivity ramp-up
- [ ] Problem-solving and initiative
- [ ] Quality and reliability

**Culture Assessment**:
- [ ] Team collaboration and relationships
- [ ] Company culture fit
- [ ] Communication and feedback reception
- [ ] Initiative and engagement
- [ ] Contribution to team goals

**Future Planning**:
- [ ] Confirm permanent role status
- [ ] Discuss career development
- [ ] Set 90+ day goals
- [ ] Identify growth opportunities
- [ ] Plan skill development

**Success Criteria** (must meet ALL):
- ✅ Performing all core job responsibilities
- ✅ Quality of work meets expectations
- ✅ Positive team relationships established
- ✅ Culture fit confirmed
- ✅ Productivity approaching full capability

**Outcomes**:
- ✅ **Pass**: Full role confirmation, no restrictions
- ⚠️ **Conditional**: Extended review with specific improvements needed
- ❌ **Not Suitable**: Role not right fit, transition plan

## Documentation Standards

### Onboarding Documentation

**Pre-Onboarding Documents**:
- Welcome email (personalized)
- Offer letter and agreement
- NDA and confidentiality agreement
- Benefits overview
- Start date confirmation

**Day 1 Documents**:
- Welcome packet with office address and parking info
- Team directory with photos and bios
- Department organization chart
- Company organization chart
- Key contacts and phone numbers

**Week 1 Documents**:
- Employee handbook (PDF)
- Code of conduct
- Security policies
- System access guide
- Training schedule and materials

**30/60/90 Day Documents**:
- Individualized 30-60-90 day plan
- Performance expectations document
- Role responsibilities checklist
- Goals and success metrics
- Development plan

### Documentation Quality Standards

All onboarding documents must:
- ✅ Be clear and accessible (non-legal language where possible)
- ✅ Include examples and visuals where helpful
- ✅ Provide clear next steps
- ✅ Include contact information for questions
- ✅ Be available in multiple formats (PDF, digital)
- ✅ Be kept confidential as appropriate

## Technology Provisioning

### Required System Access (Day 1)

**Email & Communication**:
- [ ] Email account (firstname.lastname@aurigraph.io)
- [ ] Slack account with appropriate channels
- [ ] Calendar account with team schedules
- [ ] Phone/mobile device (if applicable)

**Code & Development** (if technical role):
- [ ] GitHub account access and repository setup
- [ ] SSH key generation and storage
- [ ] IDE/development environment setup
- [ ] Local development environment configured
- [ ] Build and test system access

**Project Management**:
- [ ] JIRA account and project access
- [ ] Confluence wiki access
- [ ] Document sharing (Google Drive, etc.)
- [ ] Team board/task system access

**Infrastructure** (if applicable):
- [ ] AWS/Cloud platform accounts
- [ ] VPN access
- [ ] Network access credentials
- [ ] Physical building access card
- [ ] Parking authorization

### Access & Security Best Practices

**Account Creation**:
- [ ] Use official naming conventions (firstname.lastname)
- [ ] Generate secure temporary password
- [ ] Force password change on first login
- [ ] Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- [ ] Set up security questions and recovery options

**Access Provisioning**:
- [ ] Create access on day 1, not before
- [ ] Test all accesses before handoff to new hire
- [ ] Document all access credentials securely
- [ ] Provide access checklist to new hire
- [ ] Follow principle of least privilege (only needed access)

**Offboarding Preparation**:
- [ ] Document all access points for offboarding
- [ ] Set up access removal procedures
- [ ] Train manager on offboarding process
- [ ] Plan knowledge transfer

## Best Practices

### Pre-Onboarding Best Practices

1. **Personalization** - Customize welcome based on role and background
2. **Early Communication** - Maintain contact from offer to start date
3. **Equipment Ready** - Have all hardware ready and tested on day 1
4. **Team Preparation** - Brief team before new hire arrives
5. **Excitement Building** - Create positive anticipation for arrival

### Day 1 Best Practices

1. **Warm Welcome** - Manager and buddy greet with enthusiasm
2. **Comprehensive Tour** - Show office, facilities, resources
3. **Quick Wins** - Give early successes to build confidence
4. **Buddy Assigned** - Have peer available for informal questions
5. **Manager Check-ins** - Regular check-ins throughout day

### Week 1 Best Practices

1. **Structured Learning** - Balance training with real work
2. **Social Integration** - Include social activities and lunch
3. **Relationship Building** - Facilitate introductions to key people
4. **Clear Expectations** - Define role and success criteria
5. **Daily Check-ins** - Short, frequent manager touchpoints

### 30-Day Best Practices

1. **Project Assignment** - Give meaningful, guided projects
2. **Mentorship** - Assign mentor or use buddy system
3. **Regular Feedback** - Provide weekly constructive feedback
4. **Culture Immersion** - Include in team meetings and activities
5. **Mid-Point Check** - 15-day informal checkpoint

### 60-Day Best Practices

1. **Increasing Autonomy** - Reduce supervision gradually
2. **Skill Development** - Identify and address gaps
3. **Ownership** - Give opportunity to own projects
4. **Performance Feedback** - Formal 30-day review
5. **Adjustment** - Adjust plan based on progress and feedback

### 90-Day Best Practices

1. **Final Evaluation** - Comprehensive formal assessment
2. **Role Confirmation** - Confirm permanent position
3. **Career Planning** - Discuss future development
4. **Celebration** - Celebrate successful integration
5. **New Goals** - Set goals for ongoing development

## Metrics & Measurement

### Onboarding Success Metrics

**Timeliness Metrics**:
- All pre-onboarding documents completed on time (100%)
- All systems access provisioned by day 1 (100%)
- All day-1 activities completed (95%+)
- Compliance training completed by day 30 (100%)

**Quality Metrics**:
- 30-day evaluation "meets expectations" or higher (90%+)
- Team satisfaction with integration (8/10 or higher)
- New hire satisfaction with onboarding (8/10 or higher)
- Manager confidence in new hire (8/10 or higher)

**Productivity Metrics**:
- First code/work contribution by day 5 (80%+)
- Independent project ownership by day 30 (70%+)
- Full productivity by day 60 (70%+)
- Exceeding expectations by day 90 (50%+)

**Retention Metrics**:
- Retention at 90 days (95%+)
- Retention at 1 year (90%+)
- New hire satisfaction at 1 year (8/10 or higher)
